Just how bad is the Southern Alberta flood?

The 2013 Southern Alberta flood has a projected cost of over one billion dollars. That's more than the total cost of Albertan floods since 1970.

As relief efforts continue, it is becoming increasingly clear that the flood that took place in Alberta in June was like no other. It is, by far, the worst flood in the province's history. The Albertan government estimates that the total cost of the flood will top one billion dollars—35 percent more than the total cost of the floods that occured in the province between 1970 and 2010. Worse yet, a report from the Globe and Mail suggests that it will be many years before the province receives any of the relief money Ottawa sets aside for such disasters.
